ARTISTS INVOLVED

Justin Chen

Justin Chen is an independent photographer, drawn to moments of mundanity and wonder that makes us human.
As a photographer that predominantly photographs people and places, Lockdown and this project have been a reminder that there is beauty to be found in one’s immediate surroundings and that one really doesn’t have to go too far to find moments of honesty and truth.  

Jessica Van Wyk

Jessica Van Wyk is a Fine Arts graduate from the University of Pretoria. Majoring in photography, Jessica’s work has a specific focus on gender politics, mental health and the human condition.
Through the medium of photography, she was able to find her voice, exploring our relationship with ourselves, others and our place in the world around us. Lockdown is an especially important time to be conscious of our internal environment and the ways in which we value the different connections in our lives.
Jessica has taken part in exhibitions at numerous institutions including the Uni- versity of Pretoria, the Anton van Vouw Museum, the Pretoria Arts Association and the Pretoria Art Museum.  


TOMMY REBEARIVELO

Tommy Rabearivelo is a Malagasy fine art photographer based in Johannes- burg, South Africa. He attended the Market Photo Workshop and completed the Advanced Photography Programme in 2012.
In 2015 Tommy was selected to take part in the first Department of Arts and Culture Photography Incubator Programme. The programme provided him with practical experience and exposure to aspects of the photography industry and practice
