ARTISTS INVOLVED

Lloyd van Schalkwyk

Lloyd van Scahlkwyk was born in Potchefstroom in 1994 and finished his BA in fine arts at the University of Pretoria in 2016. Lloyd’s work draws inspiration from online culture, music videos and celebrity visualisation and constructs a language that communicates his underlying anxieties that arise from an inability to distinguish the “real” world from the virtual world.
These two artworks, entitled Cocoon vol. 2: Tiamat and Cocoon vol. 13: Ringworm, form part of a greater series titled The Cocoon which follows the adventures of the cocoon through the immateria or the realm of imagination. The cocoon acts as a type of dream avatar for myself and he has the ability to shape shift into different forms always transforming but never coming out of the cocoon. The entire series illustrates the development of the self through the imaginary and how this generation of self is always in flux and mainly rooted in the immaterial and ideas we have about ourselves. 

Ryan Hing and Jessica Van Wyk

Ryan is the creative director of the production agency, Evolve, directing projects with incredible creatives from all over South Africa.
Jessica is a photographic artist and the owner and editor of Rogue magazine. Jessica completed her fine arts degree in 2019, majoring in photography, creating self-reflective work that focuses on gender and human nature.
Ryan and Jessica’s collaboration aims at addressing certain gender biases that are damaging in the fight for equality as well as facing certain hard truths regarding gender dynamics in South Africa. This work sheds light on the stigma that men should not be allowed to be vulnerable or show affection to each other. It aims to celebrate the strength of women and their ability to overcome no matter what, while confronting the fact that women in South Africa have no other choice. 

Gabriella Marais

Gabriela was born in a small town in the middle of the mountains. She expresses her works through imagery of mostly dark forest landscapes as this is a very close topic to the life she grew up in.
Marais completed her studies of Fine art and received her degree in 2019.
Sexuality is a journey. Growing up Gabriela travelled a lot, leaving her home and searching for something in the small town she grew up in. Travels with Dolores is a representation of the journey she has taken with her own sexuality as a gay woman. Dolores, being a pet dog from childhood, would often follow along her on her travels, or sit quietly and watch the sunset, listening to the musings of a small child from the pavement in front of their home. 

Cassidy Matthysen

Cassidy Matthysen is an aspiring writer who is currently attaining her Bachelor of Arts in Creative Writing and English Literature. She dabbles in short fiction and is making her debut as an author on Rogue’s second edition dubbed “Androgyny”.
The content of her writing is highly influenced by pop and PC culture. She zooms into trying experiences of individuals she has met along the way. In doing so, she aims to actively deflect redundancy and raise social awareness on the unavoidable issues plaguing society. This is her small contribution to dismantling the patriarchy. Her process involves taking observations and anecdotes from personal experiences and regurgitating them onto the white page - rearranging them like alphabet soup.
